 
Key Points  

Carolee's expanded leadership team spans five groups — offering leads, market leads, and industry experts — each needing to run independent strategies that must also roll up into a unified Talent RP strategy trackable by Emily and Steve.  

Two core problems identified: (1) establishing regular operating cadences with structured and qualitative strategy tracking across ~30 MDs, and (2) improving consistency in messaging and content accessibility across the broader Talent organization.  

Steve emphasized any solution must be low-burden for leaders — front-and-center in Teams or SharePoint, not resembling timesheet-style reporting — and easily delegable to deputies.  

Pedro and Luca demoed a locally hosted task manager prototype as a proof-of-concept for capturing meeting notes, categorizing them, and generating structured action items in a single dashboard.  

A role-based dashboard was proposed — different views for Steve, Emily, and individual leads — fed by structured financial data and unstructured qualitative updates from MDs.  

Steve raised a critical security concern: revenue, sales, and profitability data cannot leave Accenture's walls, requiring any real solution to be hosted behind Accenture's firewall, not on third-party platforms like Netlify.  

Pedro and Luca clarified the demo is illustrative only; a production-ready version would require IT involvement and internal hosting, with the near-term goal being a compelling demo to justify that investment.  

Steve envisioned a single-page-per-leader format with four quadrants — financials, campaigns, strategic objectives, and qualitative narrative — surfaced in Teams or SharePoint, with structured data fed by his team and qualitative updates by the leads themselves.  

Reducing leader burden is the top implementation risk — solutions should allow delegation to deputies and minimize manual input, ideally translating meeting outputs directly into trackable action items.  

Emily highlighted the need for visual progress tracking (e.g., Gantt-style views) and cross-strategy intersection visibility, so interdependencies across markets, offerings, and industries can be identified.  

Steve recommended researching Accenture's existing technology stack — including Digital Worker, Amethyst, Copilot Studio, Teams Planner, and SharePoint — before building anything new, noting similar solutions may already exist internally.  

Post-call, Pedro and Luca identified three key technical blockers: Copilot Studio access for real agent integration, security and hosting compliance within Accenture's infrastructure, and Teams app embedding feasibility.  

Both agreed the strategy is to build a functional demo showing what the solution could look like, then present the steps needed to make it Accenture-compliant — hooking stakeholders on the possibility before pursuing formal approval.
